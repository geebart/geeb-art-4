Branding at Birth

How'd you get that nickname?

One day, my mother took me and my brothers to go grocery shopping. While going down the baby food aisle my little brother started acting weird. All of the sudden, he began pointing up at Gerber baby food jars on the shelf.

My mom took notice and encouraged him to speak up. In frustration, he kept pointing at the shelf and then back at me without an auditory response. Eventually, he squeaked out, "Geeber!" instead of "Gerber!". He mistakenly thought I was the baby featured on all the Gerber products.

From then on, I would be known as "Geeber" or "Geeb" for short. Little did I know, "Geeb" would later become my brand .

A True Digital Native

Born on-screen, bred online

  • 1987
    Geeb is Born
  • 1994
    Amateur Level Designer

    At age 7, I designed my first level for a video game using the Pie in the Sky game engine. This moment would help kickstart a short career in game design.

  • 1996
    Fatherly Role Model

    My dad placed in a 3D level design contest for Quake. This would further engourage me to make my own levels for Quake and other game engines.

  • 1997
    A True Quaker

    Quake 2 was the next big thing in the computer gaming world so naturally I began developing levels for it.

  • 1999
    Geeks and Gearheads

    My dad started a side business building gamming PCs for friends and family. Eventually, I took on the hobby as well. I developed two PCs from scratch, but modded them almost every year.

  • 2001
    Set Us Up the Bomb

    Counter-Strike took over the gaming world and I began creating more levels.

  • 2003
    BattleGrounds Mod

    I was selected to join the BattleGrounds Half-Life mod. The team was comprised of developers, historians and designers from all over the world. The game was free to play and was enjoyed by thousands of fans. This experience taught me about different culturals, collaboration, coding and design.

  • 2005
    Lead Level Designer

    Acknowledging my new skillset, the BG team nominated me Lead Level Designer. By the time the final build for BG1 went public, I had made contributions to all levels in the game.

  • 2005
    Off to College

    I was accepted at Illinois State University (ISU) in a new program called Arts Technology. My skill set would soon expand to include a wide range of digital arts.

  • 2008
    First Exhibited Artwork

    I was selected to submit work for Test Pattern Soundcheck, an Arts Technology exhibition. Imagery from my piece, "Code as a Pillar" was mentioned in local press.

  • 2009
    Bachelor's Degree

    By developing foundational skills in web development, sound engineering, video, programming, design and art installation, I became a jack of all trades in everything digital.

  • 2010
    The Graduate

    Like many of my fellow graduates, I entered the professional market at the worst time in decades. This queued me to consider continuned education as a career option. I decided to stay at ISU for two more years.

  • 2010
    A Grecian Scholar

    My research essay entitled, "Jason Salavon in Post-Pop" was selected to be presented at the 1st International Conference at ATINER. On behalf of ISU, I traveled to Athens, Greece to present my research. Years later, my paper was included in a book entitled, Visual and Performing Arts.

  • 2011
    Identity Reconnected

    Parterning with Philippe Moore, we put together a culminating art exhibition for our masters program. To help fund the show, we won a Friends of the Arts grant through our University and charitable members of the local art community.

  • 2011
    Master's Degree

    I recieved my Master's of Science in Arts Technology. I would hit the job market looking for work as a creative technologist and front-end developer.

  • 2011
    Front-End Developer at ARS

    My first full-time position out of school was at Aisle Rocket Studios.

  • 2012
    Lead Developer at ARS

    Back-to-front, I began to take on ARS' biggest projects. Known for being a stickler for details, I quickly positioned myself as a sudo product and project manager.

  • 2014
    Development Team Manager at ARS

    After gaining the respect of my peers and proving myself on dozens of projects, I earned a prominent leadership role in the company. My team would continue to receive compliments on our level of collaboration and execution.

  • 2016
    Dev/Platform Manager at ARS

    My responsibilites as a leader expanded. In addtion to manging the development team, I also became Platform Manager. I am now the key stakeholder for our clients largest web platforms. From the design and code to the business KPI's, I was responsible for making experiences come to life.
